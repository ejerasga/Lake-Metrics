Paoay Lake

Paoay Lake: Nature's Serenity, Where Beauty Meets Benevolence

Paoay Lake, situated within Ilocos Norte province, is bordered by five barangays. To the north lies Suba, the northeast adjoins Nanguyudan, and to the east, it meets Pasil. Its southern boundary is marked by Sungadan, and on the western side, it abuts Nagbacalan, which is in proximity to the city of Laoag.



Given its considerable size, the locals have affectionately dubbed it "Dacquel a Danum," a term in the local dialect signifying "big water." Unlike many other lakes, Paoay Lake doesn't receive water from tributaries. Its water source primarily consists of underground springs and surface runoff, which includes excess rainwater that flows from the surrounding hills. The neighboring farming communities derive substantial benefits from the lake, particularly as it serves as a vital source of irrigation for their crops.



In recognition of the lake's intrinsic natural beauty, flourishing ecosystems, and its invaluable public contributions, it was designated a National Park under Republic Act 5631 in 1961. Paoay Lake also stands as one of the premier tourist destinations in the province, cherished for its picturesque vistas and tranquil ambiance.
